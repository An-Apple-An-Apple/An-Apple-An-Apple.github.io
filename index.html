COMPUTER
GRAPHICS
Computer graphics have been in use since the inception of modern computers, both in software and hardware.

Computer graphics is a vast field that governs the presentation and communication of information from the computer to humans such that they return displayable results.

With the advancement of algorithms associated with both two-dimensional and three-dimensional graphical presentations, developers can deploy more powerful usages of computer graphics.

Computer graphics algorithms alongside acceleration of hardware allows computers to simulate and calculate seemingly impossible inquiries due to the complexity and quantity of calculation.

The calculations and simulation of computer graphics can prevent disastrous events by predicting the future movement of natural or artificial disasters by simulation and aid the development of artificial intelligence and other fields with virtual design tools.
Potentials.
Computer graphics is what people see, whether that be on phones, computers, or tablets. Computer graphics define the colors of each pixel on the screen. It defines how people interact with computers and what they show. Without its concepts, humans can not use computers and get visual results. YOU can not see this information. YOU will not see any screen. There will be no video games, no phones, no computers, no tablets. There will be no simulation in this world, and no prediction of the future; no weather app, no tide warnings, no safe experiments.

Artificial intelligence may exist but will learn 1001 slower and unusable. Designing will be even more complicated; stack upon stacks of paper made by hand. As technology develops computers will be more powerful and alongside computer graphics. AI based simulation could be real time or even faster than reality. Computers can create an alternate reality where we can simulate everything, know everything ahead of time. Disasters can be prevented and lives can be saved and improved. Computer graphics is the user interface of a computer, and the mouth of hands of a digital creature.
Simulation
Computer simulation is predictions. Based on real-world examples in the past, simulators calculate the future. Some real-world examples have been categorized into laws, such as those of aerodynamic, and heat transfer. Those laws enable simulation to be more powerful and accurate than ever before. It can now predict weather events, aircraft trajectory, and even human brains and thoughts. Those predictions allow humans to know the outcome of events before their arrival, so we can prepare and avoid catastrophes. Humans can simulate little blobs of fluids in order to predict weather.

Problems like weather prediction, drug research and design, and rocket dynamics are hard because they involve extreme scales of space or time, vast amounts of data, and potentially dangerous conditions. Computers allow us to simulate complex or dangerous environments to test hypotheses about their behavior.

Simulate how rays of particles zooming into and out of existence inside a computer inorder to simulate light and make digital images look photorealistic. We can simulate how a rock would break to know what that rock looked like 1 million years ago. To copy nature is to simulate the future. Simulations can predict how things would go in the future, but also the past. Simulation is a time machine. We can travel forward and backward. With enough calculation powers, we can simulate everything.

From the author.
Through this project, I have learned much about web development. It has mastered me in javascript, css, and git of which I have never touched before. But more, the amount of effort starting from v1.0 which looks horrendous and is uninteractive and unresponsive until the at least minimalist v3.0 has shown me that with discipline, I can do anything even if I start from the beginning as long as I take the first step. It does not matter how complicated your goals are, you have to set high standards.

It is impossible to make perfection on the first try but you can slowly build on it. If you set low standards, it is hard to create interests and find motivation. I have found through this project that I am extremely practical and learn best from actions although with strict explanations and documentations. In this project, I have learned that it is always hard for me to find reasons and motivation to start actions and thus I frequently do not, however I have learned that once I do, I can create as many fantastic creations as I want.
