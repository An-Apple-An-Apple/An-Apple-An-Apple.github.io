COMPUTER
GRAPHICS
Computer graphics have been in use since the inception of modern computers, both in software and hardware.

Computer graphics is a vast field that governs the presentation and communication of information from the computer to humans such that they return displayable results.

With the advancement of algorithms associated with both two-dimensional and three-dimensional graphical presentations, developers can deploy more powerful usages of computer graphics.

Computer graphics algorithms alongside acceleration of hardware allows computers to simulate and calculate seemingly impossible inquiries due to the complexity and quantity of calculation.

The calculations and simulation of computer graphics can prevent disastrous events by predicting the future movement of natural or artificial disasters by simulation and aid the development of artificial intelligence and other fields with virtual design tools.
Simulation.
Computer simulation is predictions. Based on real-world examples in the past, simulators calculate the future. Some real-world examples have been categorized into laws, such as those of aerodynamic, and heat transfer. Those laws enable simulation to be more powerful and accurate than ever before. It can now predict weather events, aircraft trajectory, and even human brains and thoughts. Those predictions allow humans to know the outcome of events before their arrival, so we can prepare and avoid catastrophes. Humans can simulate little blobs of fluids in order to predict weather.

Simulate how rays of particles zooming into and out of existence inside a computer inorder to simulate light and make digital images look photorealistic. We can simulate how a rock would break to know what that rock looked like 1 million years ago. To copy nature is to simulate the future. Simulations can predict how things would go in the future, but also the past. Simulation is a time machine. We can travel forward and backward. With enough calculation powers, we can simulate everything.
